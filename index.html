I am an Assistant Professor in Department of Biostatistics and Bioinformatics at Duke University.

My primary research interest is developing novel statistical and computational methods for integrating and analyzing big and complex genomic data, especially data from single-cell sequencing. Some examples include the differential analysis of single-cell RNA-sequencing (scRNA-seq) data with multiple samples, joint analysis of paired single-cell RNA and T cell receptor (TCR) sequencing data, pseudotime trajectory inference, and signal recovery for single-cell ATAC-sequencing (scATAC-seq) data. I have been actively applying these methods to various biological and clinical studies, such as immunology and cancer.

I am looking for students and postdocs to work together on exciting research projects. Please contact me if your are interested. Email: zhicheng.ji@duke.edu
